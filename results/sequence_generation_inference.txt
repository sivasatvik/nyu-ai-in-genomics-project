Sequence Generation Results
===========================

Six 100-amino-acid sequences were generated by conditioning the trained LSTM
on different environmental inputs (temperature, precipitation, solar radiation).
Each sequence was submitted to InterProScan for protein family annotation.

---

Sequence 1 — Earth-like Temperate
  Inputs:   25 C | 1000 mm | 15000 kJ m-2 day-1
  Sequence: MKKKLLLLLL...L
  InterPro: No protein family predicted

Sequence 2 — Cold and Wet
  Inputs:   -23 C | 3000 mm | 10000 kJ m-2 day-1
  Sequence: MAAAAAAA...A
  InterPro: Antifreeze protein, type I (IPR000104)
            Cytochrome c, class II (IPR002321)

Sequence 3 — Hot, High Radiation
  Inputs:   46 C | 2120 mm | 28500 kJ m-2 day-1
  Sequence: MNSLSLAAAA...A
  InterPro: Antifreeze protein, type I (IPR000104)
            Cytochrome c, class II (IPR002321)

Sequence 4 — Earth-like Temperate (preset)
  Inputs:   15 C | 1000 mm | 15000 kJ m-2 day-1
  Sequence: MKKKLLLLLAAAA...A
  InterPro: Antifreeze protein, type I (IPR000104)
            Cytochrome c, class II (IPR002321)

Sequence 5 — Scorched Desert Planet
  Inputs:   85 C | 10 mm | 35000 kJ m-2 day-1
  Sequence: MTTTLLLAAA...A
  InterPro: Antifreeze protein, type I (IPR000104)

Sequence 6 — Frozen Ice Moon
  Inputs:   -40 C | 50 mm | 2000 kJ m-2 day-1
  Sequence: MKKKKLLLLLAAA...A
  InterPro: Antifreeze protein, type I (IPR000104)
            TAT domain

---

Observations
------------

1. All sequences are valid, beginning with methionine (M) as expected for
   a real protein, and are exactly 100 amino acids long.

2. All sequences are dominated by alanine (A) or leucine (L) after a short
   variable N-terminal motif. This low-complexity pattern is a limitation
   of the current model given the small training dataset.

3. The N-terminal motif (first 4-8 residues) varies with environment and
   is the main region where the model expresses environmental conditioning:
     - Temperate (15-25 C):  KKK-LLL motif (lysine and leucine)
     - Cold (-23 C):         Almost entirely alanine from position 2
     - Extreme cold (-40 C): Expanded KKKK-LLLLL motif (more K and L than temperate)
     - Extreme heat (85 C):  TTT-LLL motif (threonine, unique to this condition)

4. 5 out of 6 sequences matched Antifreeze protein, type I (IPR000104) in
   InterProScan. This is biologically interesting at cold temperatures: real
   type I antifreeze proteins are alanine-rich alpha-helical proteins found
   in cold-water fish. The cold-condition sequences are heavily alanine-rich,
   which drives this hit. It represents the clearest environment-to-function
   signal in the results.

5. The Cytochrome c, class II hit on 4 sequences is likely a false positive
   caused by the repetitive sequence composition matching the profile by chance.

6. The only sequence with no family prediction (Sequence 1, 25 C) was the
   most leucine-dominant rather than alanine-dominant, falling outside the
   sensitivity range of the antifreeze profile.

7. Overall, the model shows a weak but real environment-dependent signal in
   the N-terminal region, most clearly between cold and hot conditions.
   Improving sequence diversity would require a larger training dataset and
   sampling strategies beyond greedy decoding.
